About Me

I am a PhD student in Mathematics at Louisiana State University (LSU), advised by Prof. Pramod Achar. My research focuses on Representation Theory, with additional interests in Algebraic Combinatorics and Data Science. I am equally passionate about teaching, mentoring students from high school to postgraduate levels.

Education

  • PhD in Mathematics – Louisiana State University (current)
  • M.Phil./M.Sc. – Indian Institutes of Technology (IIT Bombay / IIT Kanpur)
  • B.Sc. in Mathematics & Computer Science – Chennai Mathematical Institute (CMI)
  • High School – Howrah Zilla School

Projects & Thesis

  • Geometric Representation Theory (PhD thesis, LSU) – Studying perverse sheaves and Hecke algebras using Kazhdan-Lusztig theory (Advisor: Prof. Pramod Achar)
  • Machine Learning on Antifungal Drugs – Developing prediction models using RF, SVM, GBT, NN with collaborators K. Mondal, M. Pandey, G. Srivastav
  • Darboux Function and Markov Computability – IIT Kanpur, 2017 (Advisor: Prof. Satyadev Nandakumar)
  • Word & Conjugacy Problem in Braid Group – IIT Kanpur, 2016 (Advisor: Prof. Abhijit Pal)
  • Minimal Planar Oriented Cliques – ISI Kolkata, 2015 (Advisor: Prof. Sandip Das)
